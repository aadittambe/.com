👋🏽 Hey, hey! I am Aadit, a journalist and developer who tells data-driven stories visually — with code.

📍 I am currently an intern in the graphics and design departments at The Washington Post.

⬅️ Previously, I was a data fellow at the Howard Center for Investigative Journalism, where I wrote web scrapers and crunched data sets to report public-interest stories in narrative ways.

📊 Before that, I interned at NBC News on the data and graphics team — I wrote code to develop interactive graphics to tell data-driven stories visually.

✍️ Recently, I started a blog where I write about things I learn — and hacks that help me — related to data and graphics. You should check it out!

📚 I have a master’s degree in data and computational journalism from the University of Maryland. I got my undergraduate training at the University of Iowa, where I studied journalism and German.

📝 This portfolio showcases projects in which I have used computational methods to wrangle data and write — often in JavaScript, but also in English — with a goal of making the news more accessible and understandable. My complete work experience can be found in my resume.

🤝 If you like to keep up with current events and care about the news experience as a whole — or want to simply chat about trains or nerd out about antique fountain pens — we should connect!
